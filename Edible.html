Grow to Nourish, Grow to Sustain

In this page, you will explore how edible plants can support both your health and a more sustainable lifestyle. You’ll learn about different types of edible plants such as fruits, vegetables, herbs, and plant-based protein sources. For each one, we provide detailed information about the plant, including its needs and benefits, along with step-by-step guides on how to grow it — from soil preparation and planting to daily care and harvesting. You’ll also find ideas on how to reuse your harvest in eco-friendly ways, making the most of what you grow.

Strawberry

Strawberries are low-growing plants that produce sweet, juicy fruits with tiny seeds on the surface.

Growing Requirements:

  • Sunlight Needed: 8 to 10 hours of full sun daily
  • Water Needs: 0.5 per hour
  • Soil Type: Well-drained sandy loam, slightly acidic

Strawberry's

Strawberry Planting Guide:

  • Select a sunny site with well-drained sandy loam soil.
  • Remove all weeds and prepare soil by loosening and adding organic matter.
  • Purchase certified disease-free plants with healthy crowns and roots.
  • Plant crowns so roots are just covered by soil, spacing 12–18 inches apart.
  • Water immediately after planting to settle soil around roots.
  • Mulch with straw or pine needles to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
  • Pinch off flowers for 4–6 weeks after planting to encourage root establishment.
  • Maintain regular watering and weed control; harvest begins about 6 weeks after flowering.
  • After 2–3 months, enjoy your strawberry harvest!

    • Orange

    Orange trees are small evergreen citrus trees producing sweet, segmented fruits rich in vitamin C.

    Growing Requirements:

    • Sunlight Needed: 6 to 8 hours of full sun daily
    • Water Needs: 4–6 liters per hour depending on tree size
    • Soil Type: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil

    Orange's information

    Orange Planting Guide

    • Choose a sunny, frost-protected site with well-draining soil.
    • Dig a hole twice as wide and deep as the root ball.
    • Amend soil if needed with compost or sand to improve drainage.
    • Remove tree gently from pot, avoid root damage.
    • Position tree so root crown is level with soil surface.
    • Backfill hole with amended soil, pressing gently to remove air pockets.
    • Water thoroughly after planting to settle soil.
    • Mulch around base and water regularly, especially in spring and summer.
    • After 2–3 years, enjoy fresh oranges from your tree!

    Grapes

    Grapevines are woody climbing plants that produce clusters of sweet berries used fresh, or for wine.

    Growing Requirements:

    • Sunlight Needed: 6 to 8 hours of full sun daily
    • Water Needs: 2–3 liters per hour per
    • Soil Type: Well-drained loamy soil

    Grapes's information

    Grapes Planting Guide

    • Purchase dormant, bare-root grapevines from a reputable source.
    • Soak roots in water for 2–3 hours before planting.
    • Select a sunny site with support structures like a trellis or fence.
    • Dig a hole about 12 inches deep and wide, twice root system size.
    • Fill hole halfway with soil, place roots, tamp gently, then backfill.
    • Water immediately after planting.
    • Prune vines to two stems to encourage healthy growth.
    • Maintain weekly watering and prune annually to manage growth.
    • After 2–3 years, enjoy your homegrown grapes!

    Blueberry

    Blueberries are deciduous shrubs producing small, antioxidant-rich blue-black berries.

    Growing Requirements:

    • Sunlight Needed: Minimum 6 hours of full sun daily
    • Water Needs: 1.5–2 liters per hour
    • Soil Type: Acidic, well-drained sandy soil

    Blueberry's information

    Blueberry Planting Guide

    • Test and amend soil pH to 4.2–5.2 if needed.
    • Choose sunny site or raised bed with acidic, well-draining soil.
    • Dig holes and mix soil with peat moss or acidic compost.
    • Plant bushes with roots spread and crown level with soil.
    • Backfill and water thoroughly.
    • Apply 2–3 inches organic mulch to conserve moisture.
    • Water regularly to keep soil moist but not saturated.
    • Prune annually to maintain health and encourage fruiting.
    • After 2–3 years, enjoy delicious blueberries from your plants!

    Papaya

    Papaya is a fast-growing tropical tree producing sweet, orange flesh fruits often eaten fresh or in salads.

    Growing Requirements:

    • Sunlight Needed: 6 to 8 hours of full sun daily
    • Water Needs: 3 liters per hour
    • Soil Type: Well-drained sandy loam

    Papaya's

    Papaya Planting Guide:

    • Choose a sunny location with well-drained soil.
    • Plant seeds or seedlings spaced 7-10 feet apart.
    • Water regularly to keep soil moist but not waterlogged.
    • Fertilize monthly with balanced fertilizer.
    • Protect young plants from strong winds.
    • Thin fruit clusters to improve size and quality.
    • Watch for pests like aphids and treat if needed.
    • Harvest fruits when skin turns yellow-orange.
    • After 6-12 months, enjoy your fresh papayas!

    Carrot

    Carrots are root vegetables enjoyed raw, cooked, or juiced for their sweet flavor and crunch.

    Growing Requirements:

    • Sunlight Needed: 6 hours full sun daily
    • Water Needs: 1 liter per hour
    • Soil Type: Loose, sandy loam

    Carrot's information

    Carrot Planting Guide:

    • Prepare loose, deep soil free of stones.
    • Sow seeds directly 1/4 inch deep in rows 12 inches apart.
    • Thin seedlings to 2 inches apart after sprouting.
    • Keep soil consistently moist.
    • Mulch to retain moisture and prevent weeds.
    • Fertilize lightly with compost or balanced fertilizer.
    • Watch for carrot flies and pests.
    • Harvest when roots are bright orange and mature size.

    Green gram is a popular Myanmar pulse, rich in protein and used in soups, desserts, and salads.

    Growing Requirements:

    • Sunlight Needed: 6-8 hours full sun daily
    • Water Needs: 1.2 liters per hour
    • Soil Type: Well-drained sandy loam

    Green Gram's information

    Green Gram Planting Guide:

    • Sow seeds directly after last frost.
    • Space seeds 4 inches apart in rows 18 inches apart.
    • Water regularly, especially during flowering.
    • Fertilize with compost or manure.
    • Weed regularly to reduce competition.
    • Watch for pod borers and aphids.
    • Harvest when pods are dry and brown.
    • Thresh and dry beans before storage.
    • After 2-3 months, enjoy your green gram!
