Dahlia

Dahlias are vibrant tuberous flowers with large, colorful blooms, popular in Myanmar’s summer gardens.

Dahlia's

What to know about Dahlia:

Sunlight: Full sun, at least 6 hours daily.

Water: water deeply 2-3 times weekly once sprouted, keeping soil moist but not soggy.

Soil: Well-drained, fertile loamy soil.

Growth Time:Blooms in 90-120 days.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Pick a sunny spot that gets at least 6 hours of sunlight each day.
  • Make the soil loose and mix in compost or old manure to help plants grow better.
  • Wait until the weather is warm and there is no more risk of frost before planting.
  • Dig holes about 4-6 inches deep and 18 inches apart.
  • Place the tuber (root) in the hole with the “eye” facing up, then cover it with soil.
  • Do not water right away. Wait until you see green shoots coming up.
  • When shoots appear, water deeply 2-3 times a week. Keep the soil moist but not soggy.
  • When the plant is about 8 inches tall, use flower food every 2 weeks.
  • Pinch off the top of the stem when it is 12-16 inches tall to help the plant grow more branches and flowers.
  • After 12-17 weeks (3-4 months), enjoy the plant!

Aster

Asters produce daisy-like flowers and prefer cooler summer temperatures—often grown from nursery plants to ensure quicker blooms.

Aster's

What to know about Aster:

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.

Water: Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged; avoid wetting leaves.

Soil: Loamy, well-drained, slightly acidic soil.

Growth Time: 60-90 days to bloom.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Choose a spot with lots of sun or light shade (8-10 hours daily).
  • Make the soil loose and add compost for better growth.
  • Plant small aster plants or seeds in spring.
  • Leave space between each plant so roots can spread.
  • Water the soil at the base to keep it moist but not soggy. Try not to get water on the leaves.
  • Feed with flower food every 2 weeks until you see flowers.
  • Trim plants periodically to encourage new blooms.
  • Blooms in about 2-3 months (8-13 weeks).
  • After 8-13 weeks (2-3 months), enjoy the plant!
  • Every 3 years, dig up and split the plants to keep them healthy and growing well.

Verbena

Verbena is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that blooms profusely in hot summer sun. It’s often grown for its long-lasting, colorful flowers.

Verbena's

What to know about Verbena:

Sunlight: Full sun, 8-10 hours daily.

Water:Moderate; about 1 inch per week, avoid wet foliage.

Soil: Well-drained soil, tolerates poor soil if drainage is good.

Growth Time: Blooms in first season, continuous bloom with trimming.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Find a spot with full sun (8-10 hours daily).
  • Mix compost into the soil to make it rich and loose.
  • Plant seeds or small plants directly in the ground.
  • Water at the base of the plant, not on the leaves, especially if the weather is dry.
  • Mulch around the plants to keep soil moist and stop weeds.
  • Divide root clumps every 3 years to maintain plant health.
  • Trim the plants after flowers fade to help new flowers grow.
  • After 8-13 weeks (2-3 months), enjoy the plant!

Madagascar Periwinkle

Madagascar Periwinkle (Vinca) is a drought-tolerant annual with glossy leaves and bright, colorful flowers, ideal for hot summers.

Madagascar Periwinkle's

What to know about Vinca:

Sunlight: Full sun to part shade.

Water: Moderate watering; drought tolerant but needs more water in containers.

Soil: Fertile, well-drained soil enriched with organic matter.

Growth Time: Blooms in 60-90 days.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Wait until the soil is warm (at least 15°C/60°F).
  • Use soil that drains well and mix in compost.
  • Plant seeds or small plants 10-20 inches apart.
  • Water when the soil feels dry. Water more often if growing in pots.
  • Feed lightly with plant food if needed.
  • Mulch around the plants to keep the soil moist.
  • Remove old flowers to help new ones grow.
  • After 8-10 weeks (about 2 months), enjoy the plant!

Lobelia

Lobelia is a compact plant with vibrant blue, purple, or white flowers, suitable for borders and containers.

Lobelia's

What to know about Lobelia:

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade (4-6 hours sun).

Water: Keep soil moist but not waterlogged.

Soil: Rich, well-drained, slightly acidic soil (pH 6.0-7.0).

Growth Time: Blooms in 8-10 weeks.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Loosen the soil and mix in compost.
  • Plant small plants 6-18 inches apart.
  • Water well after planting and keep the soil moist.
  • Mulch to keep the soil moist and stop weeds.
  • Feed once a month.
  • Pinch the tips for bushier plants.
  • Give some shade if the weather is very hot.
  • After 8-10 weeks (about 2 months), enjoy the plant!

Digitalis (Foxglove)

Foxglove is a tall biennial or perennial with tubular flowers, often grown for its striking garden presence.

Digitalis's

What to know about Foxglove:

Sunlight: Partial shade to full sun.

Water: Moderate; avoid waterlogged soil.

Soil: Well-drained, slightly acidic soil.

Growth Time: Blooms in 70-90 days.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Plant seeds indoors or outdoors in loose, well-drained soil.
  • Thin small plants to 12-18 inches apart.
  • Water to keep the soil moist, not soggy.
  • Feed lightly with plant food.
  • Mulch to keep the soil moist and stop weeds.
  • Stake tall plants if they start to lean.
  • Wear gloves when handling (the plant can be poisonous).
  • After 10-13 weeks (about 2-3 months), enjoy the plant!

Pauk Flower (Butea monosperma)

Known as the “Flame of the Forest,” this tree bursts with bright red flowers at the start of the rainy season in Myanmar’s central regions.

Pauk Flower's

What to know about Pauk:

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.

Water: Moderate; tolerates heavy rains well.

Soil: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil.

Growth Time:Blooms after 2-3 years of growth.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Select a sunny or lightly shaded spot with well-drained soil.
  • Soak seeds overnight to soften seed coat.
  • Plant seeds 1-2 inches deep in prepared soil.
  • Water regularly to keep soil moist but avoid waterlogging.
  • Fertilize seedlings after establishment with balanced fertilizer.
  • Mulch to retain moisture during heavy rains.
  • Prune young plants lightly to encourage branching.
  • After 24-36 months, enjoy vibrant flowers during the rainy season!

Tha Ra Phi

Tha Ra Phi is an evergreen tree with fragrant flowers used in traditional cosmetics, blooming during the rainy months, near coastal regions.

Tha Ra Phi's

What to know about Tha Ra Phi:

Sunlight: Full sun.

Water: Moderate; tolerates wet soil during rainy season.

Soil: Well-drained sandy loam, rich in organic matter.

Growth Time: Blooms in 3-4 years (36-48 months).

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Fertilize annually with organic compost.
  • Mulch to conserve moisture and reduce weeds.
  • Prune dead branches to maintain health.
  • Trim plants periodically to encourage new blooms.
  • After 36-48 months, enjoy fragrant flowers during the rainy months!

Kyar Flower / Lotus

The Lotus is an iconic aquatic flower that blooms on water surfaces in Myanmar’s rainy season, holding cultural and religious importance.

Lotus's

What to know about Lotus:

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade.

Water: Requires standing water or ponds.

Soil: Muddy or silty pond bottom, rich in organic matter.

Growth Time: Blooms in 2-3 months (8-12 weeks).

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Select a pond or water body with 6-12 inch depth.
  • Plant rhizomes in mud at the pond bottom.
  • Ensure full sun exposure.
  • Maintain water level during the rainy season.
  • Remove competing weeds.
  • Fertilize pond water if needed.
  • Protect from fish or animals.
  • After 8-12 weeks, enjoy beautiful floating flowers!

Desert Rose

A succulent shrub with thick stems and bright pink or red trumpet-shaped flowers, thriving in sunny, dry conditions.

Desert Rose's

What to know about Desert Rose:

Sunlight: Full sun.

Water: Moderate; allow soil to dry between waterings.

Soil: Well-drained, sandy or cactus mix.

Growth Time: Flowers in 7–12 months from seed

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Choose a pot with drainage holes and fill with cactus or sandy soil.
  • Place the plant in a location with at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily.
  • Water thoroughly, then let the soil dry out before watering again.
  • Fertilize with a balanced, diluted fertilizer every 4–6 weeks during the growing season.
  • Prune leggy stems in early spring to encourage bushier growth.
  • Protect from temperatures below 10°C (50°F); bring indoors if needed.
  • Watch for pests like spider mites and treat promptly.
  • Repot every 2–3 years to refresh soil and inspect roots.

Myanmar Cherry Blossom

A deciduous tree with delicate pink flowers that bloom in late winter, marking the seasonal change with beauty.

Myanmar Cherry Blossom's

What to know about Cherry Blossom:

Sunlight: Full sun.

Water: Moderate; avoid waterlogged soil.

Soil: Well-drained loam.

Growth Time: Blooms in 3–5 years.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Plant saplings in open, sunny areas with good air circulation.
  • Dig a hole twice as wide as the root ball and plant at the same depth.
  • Water deeply after planting and keep soil moist but not soggy.
  • Mulch around the base to retain moisture and regulate temperature.
  • Fertilize in early spring with a balanced fertilizer.
  • Prune dead or weak branches after flowering.
  • Protect young trees from strong winds.
  • Monitor for pests and diseases, treating as needed.

Marigold

A bright, cheerful flower with orange or yellow blooms, commonly used in festivals and gardens during winter.

Marigold's

What to know about Marigold:

Sunlight: Full sun.

Water: Moderate; let soil dry between waterings.

Soil: Well-drained, slightly acidic.

Growth Time: Blooms in 8–10 weeks from seed.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Sow seeds directly in garden beds or pots in early October.
  • Cover seeds lightly with soil and water gently.
  • Keep soil moist until seedlings emerge.
  • Thin seedlings to 8–10 inches apart.
  • Water at the base to prevent fungal diseases.
  • Fertilize once at the start of flowering.
  • Deadhead spent blooms to encourage more flowers.
  • Remove any diseased or damaged leaves promptly.

Cosmos bipinnatus

A tall, airy plant with daisy-like pink, white, or red flowers that bloom profusely in cool winter weather.

Cosmos's

What to know about Cosmos:

Sunlight: Full sun.

Water: Low to moderate.

Soil: Sandy, well-drained.

Growth Time: Blooms in 8–12 weeks from seed.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Sow seeds directly outdoors in October.
  • Lightly cover with soil and water gently.
  • Thin seedlings to 12 inches apart when they are a few inches tall.
  • Water only when the soil is dry.
  • Avoid over-fertilizing, which can reduce blooms.
  • Stake tall plants to prevent flopping.
  • Deadhead regularly to prolong flowering.
  • Collect seeds for next season after blooms fade.

Winter Heath

A low-growing shrub with small pink or white bell-shaped flowers that brighten gardens throughout winter.

Winter Heath's

What to know about Winter Heath:

Sunlight: Full sun.

Water: Minimal; drought-tolerant.

Soil: Acidic, sandy.

Growth Time: Blooms in 1–2 years.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Plant in well-drained, acidic soil in a sunny spot.
  • Space plants 12–18 inches apart for good air flow.
  • Water only during dry spells.
  • Mulch with pine needles to maintain soil acidity.
  • Avoid using lime or alkaline fertilizers.
  • Prune lightly after flowering to shape.
  • Remove dead branches as needed.
  • Monitor for pests, especially in humid weather.

Rhododendron

An evergreen shrub with clusters of bright red or pink flowers, native to Myanmar’s cooler highland regions.

Rhododendron's

What to know about Rhododendron:

Sunlight: Partial shade.

Water: Moderate, prefers humidity.

Soil: Acidic, well-drained.

Growth Time: Blooms in 4–5 years from seedling.

Step-by-Step guides to grow:

  • Test soil pH (aim for 4.5–6.0).
  • Plant in partial shade, avoiding hot afternoon sun.
  • Water with rainwater to avoid soil alkalinity.
  • Mulch with pine needles to retain acidity.
  • Fertilize with rhododendron-specific feed in spring.
  • Prune lightly after flowering.
  • Protect from strong winds and frost.
  • Monitor for pests and fungal diseases.
